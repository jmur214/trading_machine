## What was decided

- **`paused → retired` is the right fix, not time-decayed weights**: The MEDIUM health_check
  finding proposed reducing the soft-pause weight from 0.25x → 0.05x over time. On reflection,
  time-decayed weights still leave the edge trading; retirement eliminates exposure entirely once
  the evidence window is clear (90 days minimum). The revival gate protects against premature
  retirement if the edge actually recovers.
- **90-day minimum hold before retiring from pause**: Matches the retirement_min_days for
  active→retire. Gives the revival gate time to observe recovery. Short pauses (days to weeks)
  shouldn't trigger retirement — they should trigger revival evaluation.
- **PEAD 4 trades / 321 eligible events is expected, not a bug**: PEAD signal max = 0.4 at
  weight 0.5; atr_breakout emits 0.3-0.5 at weight 0.625. PEAD fires for all 321 events but
  gets outweighed in edge attribution. PEAD IS contributing to sizing — it just doesn't "win"
  the attribution label in most cases.
- **macro_credit_spread_v1 barely fires in 2025**: BAA-AAA spread only breaches its ±1 std
  threshold in Oct 2025 and Mar 2026 (tariff/volatility spikes). The edge is working correctly;
  it's a low-frequency regime signal by design.

## What was learned

- **The soft-pause drain pattern**: Paused edges with strong signals still generate enormous
  trade volume even at 0.25x weight. atr_breakout at 0.25x still did 3082 fills in 15 months.
  The lifecycle needed a "give up and retire" path, not just "keep surviving."
- **Backtest-time vs real-time timestamps**: Lifecycle events are stamped with the backtest's
  `as_of` date (last bar of the run). So `pause_date = 2024-12-31` in the history means the
  pause was triggered at the end of the 2021-2024 run, not at the real-world date the code was
  run. `days_since_pause` computed against the OOS run's `as_of` correctly reflects how long the
  edge has been paused in the BACKTEST TIMELINE, which is what matters.
