# This is a major recalibration — but in a *good* way for the project

The arc since I last looked is significant. Let me name what actually happened:

## The OOS gauntlet ran. The headline died. The diagnostics resurrected something better.

**Phase 2.10b (the test I flagged as the most important pending data point) failed three ways:**

| Test | Result | Verdict |
|------|--------|---------|
| Q1: 2025 OOS Sharpe (prod 109) | **-0.049** vs criterion >0.5 | **FAIL** — SPY did +0.955 the same year |
| Q2: Universe-B held-out 50 | **0.225** vs criterion >0.74 | **FAIL** — 79% collapse from in-sample 1.063 |
| Q3: volume_anomaly + herding standalone | **Sharpe 0.32 / -0.26** | **FAIL** — both miss benchmark threshold |

**The 1.063 was an artifact.** In-sample + favorable universe + ensemble-only behavior. In 2025, the system *lost money* in a year SPY was up ~25%. Had this been live, you'd have a ~$25k hole in a $100k account that an SPY index fund didn't have.

**That's the bad news.** Don't soften it.

## But Phase 2.10c found something genuinely important

Running three parallel diagnostics instead of throwing in the towel was the right call. The diagnosis is sharper than yesterday's "no alpha":

### Real alphas exist
- **`volume_anomaly_v1`**: positive every single year 2021–2025, +1.93% to +4.94%/yr. Stable, every-regime.
- **`herding_v1`**: positive every year, +0.55% to +2.43%/yr.

These are real. They survived 5 years of OOS by themselves. The factor decomp wasn't lying.

### The architectural defects that buried them
1. **Capital rivalry.** Bottom-3 edges consumed **83%** of fills in 2025, lost $5,645. Top-2 *good* edges got 4.3% of fills. **Un-pausing the bad edges flipped `volume_anomaly` from +$10.12 to -$1.17 per fill.** The good edges aren't bad — they're being *starved of fills* by bad edges that fire more often.

2. **Soft-pause weight leak.** `low_vol_factor_v1` fired 1,613 times in 2025 *despite being paused*, because `regime_gate` amplified it in stressed regimes — i.e., it traded most aggressively *exactly when it should NOT*. -2.53% in 2025 alone from a "paused" edge.

3. **No regime-aware slot reduction.** April-2025 `market_turmoil` produced **-$3,551 of simultaneous correlated loss across 5 edges in one month** (122% of the full-year loss). The portfolio engine has no primitive to reduce concurrent positions in stress regimes.

### The ensemble alpha paradox (genuine architectural insight)
Q3's failure of `volume_anomaly`/`herding` *standalone* wasn't proof they don't work — it was a measurement mismatch. **Standalone tests give each fill the full `risk_per_trade_pct`, which crosses the Almgren-Chriss impact knee. Ensemble fills split capital across ~17 firing signals, stay sub-knee, costs stay small.** The gauntlet's Gate 1 may actually be testing the wrong thing for ensemble-deployed edges. **That's a real and load-bearing finding.**

### What this means for the architecture

**The MetaLearner that's already shipped (default OFF) is exactly the structural fix needed.** The capital-rivalry problem is the canonical failure mode of linear `weighted_sum` allocation: every edge gets equal-vote regardless of recent performance, regime fitness, or fill quality. **A gradient-boosted meta-learner would naturally suppress the rivalry edges** because they have negative SHAP contribution — it would learn "when bottom-3 edges fire, position size should approach zero."

**Phase 2.10d (pruning + capital allocation fix) is correctly prioritized.** But the fix has two flavors:

- **Tactical (this week):** Hard-prune the 9–11 noise edges, fix the regime_gate amplification bug for paused edges, add a regime-aware slot cap. ~1–2 weeks of work. Probably gets 2025 OOS Sharpe from -0.049 to +0.2-0.4.

- **Strategic (this month):** Enable the meta-learner with per-ticker training (Session N+1.5). This addresses the *root cause* — the linear allocator isn't fit for purpose, never was, and patching it is rearranging deck chairs. The meta-learner is the actual fix.

**Don't do tactical without scheduling strategic.** Otherwise the next regime change surfaces a new variant of the same bug class.

# Yes — three material plan revisions are warranted

The Phase 2.10c findings invalidate parts of my prior plan and crystallize others. Here's what I'd change.

## Insert Phase 1.5 — Structural cleanup BEFORE anything else moves

This is the team's "Phase 2.10d" and it's now **blocking** every downstream phase. The capital-rivalry problem means *no architectural improvement matters* until the allocator stops wasting alpha. Ordering:

1. **Hard-prune the 9–11 noise edges.** Cut them from the active set entirely. Don't soft-pause; archive.
2. **Fix the soft-pause weight leak.** `regime_gate` was amplifying paused edges in stressed regimes — the exact wrong direction. Treat soft-pause as hard-multiplicative-zero in stressed regimes, not the current additive interaction.
3. **Add regime-aware slot reduction.** When regime classifier says `market_turmoil` or `crisis`, cap concurrent active positions to N/2. The April-2025 -$3,551 single-month loss across 5 correlated edges should be impossible by construction, not avoidable by diligence.
4. **Per-edge participation floor.** No single edge can consume >X% of fills regardless of its weight. Hard ceiling. The 83% fill-share for losing edges should be architecturally impossible.
5. **Mandatory: 2025 OOS rerun after these four fixes.** This becomes the new most-important data point. Expected: -0.049 → +0.2 to +0.4 Sharpe. If it doesn't move, the diagnoses are wrong.

**Until Phase 1.5 ships and 2025 OOS comes back honestly above zero, Phases 2/3/4/5/6 are all premature.** Don't add edges to a broken allocator.

## Reform Gate 1 of the gauntlet — the ensemble alpha paradox

The Q3 standalone-gauntlet failure of `volume_anomaly` and `herding` was a **false negative**. The current Gate 1 tests edges *alone* with full `risk_per_trade_pct` per fill, which crosses the Almgren-Chriss impact knee. In production they trade *as ensemble members* with split capital, sub-knee fills, and they work.

This is a load-bearing finding. The implication: **the gauntlet is currently rejecting real alphas** because the test geometry doesn't match deployment geometry.

**New Gate 1: ensemble-simulation gate.** Candidate edges get tested *inside* a simulated ensemble of the current active set, with realistic capital splitting. Standalone Sharpe becomes one diagnostic among several, not a gate. **Otherwise the gauntlet keeps generating false negatives that bury real alpha.**

This is a non-trivial change — needs careful design. But it's worth doing once, properly, before more candidates get rejected for the wrong reason.

## The MetaLearner enable timing flips

Previously I said: enable the meta-learner once per-ticker training (N+1.5) ships.

Updated: **the meta-learner enable becomes the strategic answer to the capital-rivalry problem itself.** Phase 1.5's tactical fixes (pruning, regime caps) buy time. The meta-learner is the *structural* answer because it natively suppresses bad edges via negative SHAP weights — capital rivalry is a *symptom* of the linear allocator, and patching the linear allocator is rearranging chairs.

So: Phase 1.5 (tactical) → 2025 OOS rerun (verify) → MetaLearner per-ticker (N+1.5) → enable in production with shadow comparison. The order matters because trying to enable the meta-learner *before* Phase 1.5 means you're training on contaminated data (the bad edges' fills). **Clean the data first, then learn from it.**
